A Murder Mystery for 6 Players + 1 Host

Dead at Blackpine Lodge

One night. No signal. No way out.
✦
🦉
The Setting
Blackpine Lodge
A Remote Cabin — Somewhere in the Quebec Wilderness
It is Saturday morning. The fire has gone cold. The roads are iced over. The phones have no signal. Victor Crane — your host, your rival, your benefactor, your tormentor — was found dead in his study at 7:15am by the caretaker, Tom Briggs. A locked door. A half-empty wine glass. And six people who all had reason to want him gone.

The nearest town is 40 kilometers away. No one is coming. You will need to figure this out yourselves.

The Victim
Victor Crane
Age 52 · Tech Entrepreneur · Owner of Blackpine Lodge

Self-made, brilliant, ruthless. Victor built a tech empire and made enemies at every step. He invited everyone here this weekend for what he called a "farewell gathering." Nobody knew what he meant. Now nobody can ask him.

He was found slumped in the leather armchair in his locked study. A wine glass beside him. Cause of death: acute cardiac arrest — likely induced by poisoning.

How to Play

The Structure

Setup
Host reads the opening scene aloud. Distribute character booklets (one per player). Allow 10 minutes to read privately.
Act 1
Introductions & Open Evidence (~35 min) — Each player introduces their character and reveals their physical clue. Host reveals Round 1 clues.
Act 2
Paired Interrogations (~40 min) — Run three 12-minute pairings (host assigns) plus transition time. Everyone must pressure someone outside their obvious rival.
Act 3
Full-Table Crossfire (~35 min) — Open accusations, rebuttals, and timeline reconstruction. Host reveals Round 2 clues midway.
Break
Intermission (~10 min) — Out-of-character reset. Host quietly checks if anyone is stuck and seeds pressure questions for restart.
Act 4
Final Pressure (~25 min) — Host reveals Round 3 clues one by one. Players each get a 90-second closing argument.
Act 5
The Accusation (~25 min) — Secret written accusations (who/how/why/evidence), dramatic readout, then solution reveal.
Total
~170–180 minutes (target: 3 hours)

The Rules

✦ Stay in character as much as possible — it's more fun for everyone.
✦ You may lie about your secret, your alibi, your feelings — but not about your physical clue.
✦ The murderer should not confess until the accusation round.
✦ Have drinks. Use candles. Lean into the atmosphere.

For the Host's Eyes Only

Host Guide

As host, you know who the murderer is. Your role is to facilitate, not to play a character. Keep the mystery coherent, keep pressure moving, and protect the 3-hour pacing.

Before the Game

Print each character's page and fold it so the "Your Secret" section faces inward — or simply have each player navigate to their own tab privately. Make sure each player only reads their own character sheet before Round 1.

Prepare one "physical clue" per character — a written note, printout, or object to represent it. These are listed in each character tab. You can write them on index cards.

Set a visible timer. If a scene drifts, interrupt in-character with new evidence. This scenario is tuned for six suspects + one host; avoid adding extra characters.

Opening Scene — Read This Aloud

"Last night's dinner was long and the wine flowed freely. Victor toasted to old friendships and new beginnings. Everyone went to bed at their own hour.

This morning, at 7:15am, Tom Briggs — the caretaker — noticed that the light in the study had been on all night. He knocked. No answer. He used his spare key and found Victor in the armchair, still dressed, cold to the touch. The wine glass on the side table was half full.

Dr. Owen Hale confirmed it: Victor Crane is dead. The cause is not natural. The roads are ice. The phones are dead. You are all here. And one of you did this."

Key Facts to Keep Straight

Time of Death
Approximately 12:15am Saturday. Victor was last seen alive at ~11:50pm.
Cause of Death
Potassium chloride (KCl) dissolved in his wine. Causes cardiac arrest. Colorless and nearly tasteless in wine.
The Locked Door
The study was locked from the inside. The key was in Victor's own jacket pocket. (Sylvia exited through the French window, which latched behind her — Tom can confirm the window was ajar this morning.)
The Murderer
SYLVIA MARSH. She stole KCl from Owen's bag, met Victor at 11:30pm, poisoned his wine when he turned to the window, and exited via the side French door.
False Alibi
Tom Briggs also lied — he WAS near the lodge around 11:55pm (Nadia saw him). But he's innocent; he was just checking on the building out of grief over the sale.

Tips for a Great Game

✦ If crossfire slows down, force a 60-second answer limit and reveal the next clue early.
✦ Encourage players to move around the room as their characters.
✦ If someone accidentally reveals the murderer early, have Sylvia deny it convincingly — it's part of her character!
✦ Keep a strict clock. Short urgency speeches from the host are better than long dead air.
✦ The accusation round works best with dramatic readings aloud by each player.

📄 Physical Clue You Hold

Your medical bag — and specifically, the KCl bottle inside it. You can show it to others. It is half empty, and you cannot explain why.

How She Did It

~9:00pm
While the group was at dinner and the hallway unattended, Sylvia removed a lethal quantity of potassium chloride from Dr. Hale's medical bag. She had looked it up: colorless, tasteless in wine, causes cardiac arrest. Nearly impossible to detect without a targeted autopsy.
11:30pm
She went to the study as Victor had noted in his calendar. She made one final appeal: drop the lawsuit, let her live her life. He refused. He laughed.
11:45pm
When Victor turned to look out the window, she dissolved the KCl into his wine glass and stirred it with her finger. She refilled her own glass to appear normal.
11:50pm
She made a polite excuse, said goodnight, and exited via the French window on the east wall — which she pulled shut behind her (it latched, but not locked). She wiped her wine glass clean before leaving.
~12:15am
Victor Crane's heart stopped. He was alone.

Why She Did It

Three days before the weekend, Sylvia received legal papers: Victor had filed a new civil suit seeking $4.7M in damages and a criminal fraud referral. If it proceeded, she would face bankruptcy and prison. She had spent six years rebuilding her life. Victor was taking it away again — for sport.

She had tried every legal avenue. She had no money left to fight. This was the only way she could see.

The Clues That Proved It

Calendar
"S.M. — study — 11:30pm" placed her in the room with the victim.
Fingerprint
On the wiped wine glass she thought she had cleaned thoroughly.
The Lie
She claimed to have "moved on" from the lawsuit — but the papers were three days old.
The Witness
Tom saw her exit the French window around 11:55pm — calm, alone.
The Medication
The KCl stolen from Owen's bag was the murder weapon. Sylvia had the knowledge and the access.
Victor's Text
"She's here. Pathetic. Nothing she can do." — sent 2 minutes before her scheduled meeting. His last words.

Everyone Else Was Innocent

Maggie — genuinely asleep. The sleeping pill was real. She had motive but not opportunity.
Owen — did not know his medication was taken. Asleep, likely guilty of nothing beyond the blackmail he endured.
Rex — walked outside angry, but never entered the study. His alibi gap was suspicious, not fatal.
Nadia — walked past the window, heard voices, and kept going. She didn't know what she'd heard.
Tom — sat behind the lodge out of grief for a place he loved. He saw Sylvia leave and said nothing because he feared being blamed.
